History
THE BACKSTORY Sci-fi writers and dreamers have long envisioned ways to travel at high speeds through low-pressure tubes. Rocketry pioneer Robert Goddard in 1909 proposed a vacuum train very similar in concept to the Hyperloop. In 1972, the RAND Corp. conceived a supersonic underground railway called the Vactrain. The idea was waiting for the right combination of talent, technology, and business case to become a reality.
SEEDING THE IDEA Traveling together on a humanitarian mission to Cuba, venture investor and entrepreneur Shervin Pishevar and Elon Musk get to talking about Musk’s idea for the Hyperloop, an update on the idea of moving vehicles at high speeds through low-pressure tubes. A few months later at a tech conference Shervin urges Musk to share his ideas with the public.
HYPERLOOP WHITE PAPER Elon Musk publishes the Hyperloop Alpha white paper which incites overwhelming industry excitement. Shervin presents it to President Obama, whose science advisors say it’s feasible. Obama says, "Let me know how I can help you."
HYPERLOOP TECHNOLOGIES Hyperloop Technologies, Inc. is started in a garage in Los Angeles' Los Feliz neighborhood. The first elements of a Hyperloop system and initial business model are outlined on a whiteboard by co-founders Josh Giegel and Shervin Pishevar.
LOS ANGELES INNOVATION CAMPUS Hyperloop Technologies establishes an Innovation Campus in LA's downtown arts district. The industrial space, surrounded by artists, graffiti, and gritty railroad tracks, serves as an ideal location to innovate, design, and test the components of the Hyperloop.
BIG TUBE The Big Tube is delivered to the LA campus and installed in the courtyard. The three-and-a-half ton carbon steel vessel was used to validate welds, openings, tube design, vacuum pass-throughs, and manufacturing automation. At 50 feet long and twelve feet wide, the Big Tube took up a sizable chunk of real estate in our original backyard.
LEVITATION RIG The Lev Rig is another unique test stand designed and built by the Hyperloop One team. The test stand is housed in an 18 cubic meter environment chamber that is capable of achieving pressures down to 1/1000 of atmospheric pressure. The rotor achieves surface speeds of up to 300 meters per second. These speeds are necessary to simulate Hyperloop cutting edge levitation systems for use on our test vehicles.
BLADE RUNNER The Blade Runner is installed at the Los Angeles Innovation Campus, and is the only known test rig of its kind. It was designed, fabricated and built by the Hyperloop One team to test scaled axial compressor blades and aerodynamic structures in environments down to 1/1000 of atmospheric pressure. Powered by two 2,000 CFM vacuum pumps, the Blade Runner lets the Hyperloop One team conduct long-duration tests while adjusting flow variables in speeds ranging from subsonic through supersonic regimes.
APEX The company establishes the Apex Test And Safety site in the desert outside of North Las Vegas, Nevada.
POAT At a live Propulsion System Open Air (POAT) test, a Hyperloop sled accelerates to 136 mph in 2.2 seconds, braking into a sand bank and validating the design of the motor and power electronics system. On the same day, the company launches its Hyperloop One Global Challenge to cast the widest net to find the best Hyperloop projects in the world.
METALWORKS Metalworks opens in North Las Vegas, Nevada and becomes the first Hyperloop manufacturing plant in the world. The 105,000 square foot facility serves as a tooling and fabrication plant to create Hyperloop components.
DEVLOOP CONSTRUCTION BEGINS The company breaks ground on Development Loop (DevLoop), the world's first full-scale Hyperloop test track in Apex, Nevada.
DEVLOOP FIRST TUBE INSTALLED The first tube of DevLoop, known as the Fixity, is laid into place at the Hyperloop test track in Apex, Nevada.
DEVLOOP IMAGES RELEASED With DevLoop construction complete, the first images of the first full-scale Hyperloop test track are revealed at the Middle East Rail event in Dubai, UAE.
SUCCESSFUL TEST Hyperloop One becomes the first company in the world to test a full-scale Hyperloop. It tested the combined Hyperloop components including vacuum, propulsion, levitation, sled, control systems, tube, and structures.
VEHICLE UNVEILED The first-generation Hyperloop One aeroshell is revealed. The shell is used at the DevLoop test site in Nevada to test aerodynamics and structural materials.
STATE 2 TESTING Hyperloop One achieved historic test speeds traveling nearly the full distance of the 500-meter DevLoop track in the Nevada desert. The Hyperloop One XP-1, the company’s first-generation pod, accelerated for 300 meters and glided above the track using magnetic levitation before braking and coming to a gradual stop
PROJECTED FIRST HYPERLOOP SYSTEM The company is working aggressively to meet a goal of having three production systems in service by 2021.
